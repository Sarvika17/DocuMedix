Enter the name of the medicine to get detailed information.

Antibiotics are medications used to treat bacterial infections.
They work by either killing the bacteria or inhibiting their growth.
Common examples include amoxicillin, penicillin, and ciprofloxacin.

Painkillers are drugs that alleviate pain.
They can be over-the-counter (OTC) or prescription-based.
Examples include acetaminophen, ibuprofen, and aspirin.

Antihistamines are used to treat allergies by blocking the effects of histamine,
a substance the body releases in response to allergens.
Loratadine and cetirizine are popular antihistamines.

Anti-inflammatories reduce inflammation and can be used for conditions like arthritis.
Common examples are ibuprofen and naproxen.

Antidepressants are prescribed to manage symptoms of depression and anxiety.
They work by affecting neurotransmitters in the brain.
Examples include fluoxetine, sertraline, and venlafaxine.

Antacids neutralize stomach acid and are used to relieve heartburn and indigestion.
Common ingredients include calcium carbonate and magnesium hydroxide.

Medications to control blood pressure are prescribed for conditions like hypertension.
ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, and diuretics are common classes.
